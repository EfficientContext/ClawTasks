ARTICLE 2 — SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 Party B shall provide cloud infrastructure and platform engineering services to Party A in accordance with the specifications set forth in Schedule A. The services shall include system design and architecture, development and coding, unit testing and integration testing, user acceptance testing support, deployment and go-live support, post-deployment monitoring, performance optimization, and ongoing maintenance and support throughout the Term.

2.2 Party B shall assign qualified personnel with appropriate skills, experience, and certifications to perform the Services. All personnel assigned to the project shall have a minimum of three (3) years of relevant professional experience in cloud infrastructure and platform engineering and shall hold appropriate professional certifications as required by industry standards. Party B shall provide Party A with resumes and qualification details of all key personnel prior to their assignment. Party A shall have the right to request the replacement of any personnel who, in Party A's reasonable judgment, do not meet the required standards of competence or professionalism.

2.3 Party B shall establish and maintain a project management office (PMO) to oversee the delivery of Services, including but not limited to: weekly status reports with updated Gantt charts and burn-down charts, risk registers with mitigation plans reviewed bi-weekly, change management processes with impact assessments for all proposed changes, quality assurance procedures including code review standards and testing protocols, resource management including capacity planning and skills matrix, communication protocols including escalation paths for issues of varying severity, and document management including version control and archival policies.

2.4 Party B shall provide Party A with a dedicated account manager who shall serve as the primary point of contact for all matters related to this Agreement. The account manager shall be available during Business Hours and shall respond to all inquiries within four (4) business hours. For Severity 1 issues, the account manager shall be available twenty-four (24) hours a day, seven (7) days a week. The account manager shall have sufficient authority to make operational decisions on behalf of Party B and shall attend monthly governance meetings with Party A's stakeholders.

2.5 All deliverables shall be subject to Party A's acceptance testing procedures as set forth in Schedule C. Party A shall have fifteen (15) business days to complete acceptance testing following delivery of each Deliverable. If Party A identifies any defects, non-conformities, or deviations from the specifications, Party A shall notify Party B in writing, and Party B shall correct such issues within ten (10) business days at no additional cost to Party A. If the corrected Deliverable still fails to meet the acceptance criteria after two (2) rounds of corrections, Party A shall have the right to terminate the applicable Statement of Work and receive a full refund of all fees paid for the non-conforming Deliverable.

2.6 Party B shall implement and follow industry-standard software development lifecycle (SDLC) practices, including agile or waterfall methodologies as agreed upon by the parties, version control using Git or equivalent systems, continuous integration and continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipelines, automated testing frameworks with minimum code coverage of eighty percent (80%), and documentation standards compliant with IEEE or equivalent standards.

2.7 Party B shall conduct regular knowledge transfer sessions to Party A's technical team, ensuring that Party A's personnel are capable of operating and maintaining the delivered systems independently upon completion of the engagement. Knowledge transfer shall include comprehensive documentation, hands-on training sessions, and a transition period of no less than thirty (30) days.

In the event of termination for convenience by Party A, Party A shall pay Party B for: (a) all Services satisfactorily performed through the effective date of termination; (b) any non-cancelable commitments reasonably made by Party B in connection with the Services prior to receipt of the termination notice; and (c) reasonable wind-down costs incurred by Party B in connection with the orderly cessation of Services, not to exceed ten percent (10%) of the remaining contract value.

3.3 Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail; (b) becomes insolvent, files a petition for bankruptcy, has a receiver or trustee appointed for its assets, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has any similar proceedings initiated against it; (c) ceases to conduct business in the normal course; (d) is acquired by, merges with, or undergoes a change of control in favor of a competitor of the non-terminating party; or (e) is found to be in violation of applicable anti-corruption, anti-bribery, or sanctions laws.

3.4 Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement for any reason, Party B shall: (a) immediately cease all work under this Agreement except as directed by Party A for orderly wind-down; (b) deliver to Party A all completed and in-progress Deliverables, work product, documentation, and materials in their then-current state; (c) return or, at Party A's election, securely destroy all Confidential Information of Party A, and certify such destruction in writing; (d) provide reasonable transition assistance for a period of up to ninety (90) days at Party B's then-current rates; (e) cooperate with Party A and any successor service provider to ensure an orderly transition; and (f) submit a final invoice for all outstanding fees within thirty (30) days of the effective date of termination.

3.5 The following provisions shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement: Article 1 (Definitions), Article 5 (Intellectual Property), Article 6 (Confidentiality), Article 7 (Data Protection), Article 8 (Warranties), Article 9 (Limitation of Liability), Article 10 (Indemnification), Article 11 (Dispute Resolution), and any other provisions that by their nature are intended to survive.
